# JPY Assumption Map

## Reasoning Chain

```text
Evidence → Assumption → Thesis → Investment implication → Portfolio decision
```

Never skip directly from news to a trade conclusion.

## A1 — Federal Reserve remains restrictive

- Importance: ★★★★★
- Evidence: Fed rate, U.S. CPI, employment, FOMC guidance, 2Y yield
- Strengthens: Higher for longer; sticky inflation
- Weakens: Aggressive cuts; recession; rapid disinflation
- Implications: Weak JPY; strong USD; favorable carry

## A2 — BOJ tightens gradually

- Importance: ★★★★★
- Evidence: Policy rate, guidance, wages, services CPI, outlook report
- Strengthens: Patient normalization
- Weakens: Rapid repeated hikes; durable domestic inflation
- Implications: Low yen funding cost; exporter support

## A3 — Carry remains attractive

- Importance: ★★★★★
- Evidence: Yield spread, volatility, credit, funding, positioning
- Strengthens: Wide spreads; stable liquidity
- Weakens: Deleveraging; funding stress; high volatility
- Implications: Persistent yen selling; risk-asset support

## A4 — No sustained intervention

- Importance: ★★★★☆
- Evidence: MOF statements, confirmed intervention records
- Strengthens: Verbal warnings only
- Weakens: Repeated coordinated action
- Implications: Higher short-term yen volatility

## A5 — Global markets remain stable

- Importance: ★★★★☆
- Evidence: VIX, credit spreads, banking stress, liquidity
- Strengthens: Stable markets
- Weakens: Systemic shock
- Implications: Lower carry-unwind risk

## A6 — Japanese capital continues overseas

- Importance: ★★★★☆
- Evidence: Pension allocation, insurer activity, portfolio flows
- Strengthens: Rising foreign-asset purchases
- Weakens: Sustained repatriation
- Implications: Structural yen selling

## Escalation

- No assumptions changed → no portfolio change.
- One changed → increase monitoring.
- Two changed → reassess thesis.
- Three or more changed → review exposure.
