# Japanese Investment Opportunity Playbook

## Principle

Macro tailwinds identify research areas. They do not justify an investment by themselves.

Use this sequence:

```text
Macro thesis → Sector → Business quality → Valuation → Decision
```

## Opportunity Matrix

| Area | Weak-Yen Benefit | Primary Risks | Metrics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trading houses | Foreign and commodity earnings | Commodity cycle; allocation | ROE; buybacks; dividends |
| Export manufacturers | Translation and competitiveness | Tariffs; recession; hedging | Foreign revenue; margins |
| Gaming and IP | Global revenue; scalable IP | Product cycle; execution | Digital mix; software sales |
| Technology equipment | Global demand; high-value products | Semiconductor cycle; China | Orders; backlog; margins |
| Tourism | Greater foreign purchasing power | Travel shocks; capacity | Arrivals; occupancy; spending |
| Banks | Policy normalization; wider margins | Credit losses; rapid tightening | NIM; loan growth; capital |
| Domestic retail | Usually negative import pressure | Weak consumers; input costs | Pricing power; margins |
| Utilities | Import-cost exposure | Fuel prices; regulation | Fuel mix; pricing recovery |

## Company Filter

Research only when several are present:

- Durable competitive advantage
- High-quality management
- Rational capital allocation
- Strong cash generation
- Reasonable valuation
- Genuine macro benefit
- Acceptable downside under a stronger yen

## Required Questions

- How much revenue is foreign?
- Which costs remain yen-denominated?
- Is the currency benefit hedged?
- Is the benefit already priced in?
- Would the company remain attractive under a stronger yen?
- What company-specific risk dominates the macro tailwind?
