# Bank of Japan Policy Playbook

## Objective

Distinguish routine policy management from structural regime change.

## BOJ Decision Hierarchy

### 1. Communication

Language changes may precede action.

Monitor:

- Governor statements
- Forward guidance
- Outlook-report language
- Inflation-risk balance

### 2. Policy Adjustment

A small rate change may represent normalization without regime change.

Evaluate:

- Surprise versus expectations
- Guidance after the decision
- Pace implied for future moves
- Market-function concerns

### 3. Policy Regime Change

Potentially thesis-changing evidence:

- Repeated rapid hikes
- Persistent hawkish guidance
- Durable wage-driven inflation
- Meaningfully positive real rates
- Reduced priority on financial stability

## Core BOJ Questions

- Is inflation domestic and durable?
- Is wage growth broad and persistent?
- Is services inflation strengthening?
- Is the BOJ willing to tolerate bond volatility?
- Is policy moving faster than markets expected?
- Is financial stability constraining tightening?

## Interpretation Matrix

| BOJ Development | Typical Interpretation | Thesis Response |
|---|---|---|
| No change | Neutral | No change |
| Small expected hike | Gradual normalization | Review guidance |
| Unexpected large hike | Hawkish surprise | Increase monitoring |
| Repeated rapid hikes | Potential regime change | Reassess A2 and A3 |
| More hawkish language | Early warning | Watch next meeting |
| Higher inflation forecast | Context-dependent | Test wage persistence |
| Financial-stability concern | Slower tightening risk | Supports thesis |

## Common Errors

- Treating every hike as thesis invalidation
- Ignoring the expected policy path
- Focusing only on headline CPI
- Ignoring wages and services inflation
- Assuming verbal hawkishness guarantees action
- Ignoring bond-market and fiscal constraints
