# JPY Carry Trade Playbook

## Investment Principle

Carry trades support yen weakness only while three conditions remain true:

1. Yen funding remains inexpensive.
2. Foreign yields remain meaningfully higher.
3. Volatility and funding stress remain manageable.

## Mechanics

```text
Borrow JPY
   ↓
Sell JPY
   ↓
Buy higher-yielding foreign assets
   ↓
Earn yield differential
```

This creates recurring yen selling rather than a one-time currency adjustment.

## Primary Participants

- Hedge funds
- Banks
- Macro funds
- Asset managers
- Insurers
- Pension funds
- Retail investors

## Supportive Conditions

- Wide U.S.–Japan yield spread
- Predictable central-bank policy
- Low FX volatility
- Stable credit markets
- Abundant liquidity
- Strong global risk appetite

## Warning Conditions

- Unexpected BOJ tightening
- Aggressive Fed easing
- Rapid volatility increase
- Credit deterioration
- Funding-market stress
- Geopolitical shock
- Forced deleveraging

## Carry-Unwind Sequence

```text
Risk rises
   ↓
Leverage declines
   ↓
Foreign assets are sold
   ↓
JPY is repurchased
   ↓
Yen appreciates
   ↓
More positions unwind
```

## Monitoring Questions

- Has the interest differential narrowed?
- Has volatility risen persistently?
- Is funding becoming less reliable?
- Is positioning unusually crowded?
- Is deleveraging voluntary or forced?
- Are yen purchases broad or temporary?

## Interpretation Rules

- Yen depreciation alone does not prove carry expansion.
- A temporary yen rally does not prove carry collapse.
- One-day volatility spikes are low-value evidence.
- Multiple aligned stress indicators deserve escalation.
