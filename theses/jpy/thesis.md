# JPY Structural Investment Thesis

## Metadata

- Thesis ID: JPY-001
- Horizon: 12–24 months
- Status: Active
- Last updated: July 2026
- Review cadence: Weekly scan; monthly review; quarterly reassessment

## Core Thesis

The Japanese yen is likely to remain structurally weak because:

- U.S.–Japan interest-rate differentials remain wide.
- Bank of Japan normalization remains gradual.
- Yen-funded carry trades remain attractive.
- Japanese investors continue allocating capital overseas.
- No durable catalyst yet supports sustained yen appreciation.

This is not a prediction of uninterrupted depreciation.

## Structural Drivers

### Interest-Rate Differential

Higher U.S. yields attract capital toward dollar-denominated assets.

Monitor:

- Federal Reserve policy rate
- Bank of Japan policy rate
- U.S.–Japan 2-year spread
- U.S.–Japan 10-year spread

### Carry Trade

Low-cost yen funding supports purchases of higher-yielding foreign assets.

Monitor:

- Yield spreads
- Volatility
- Funding stress
- Positioning
- Carry-unwind signals

### Bank of Japan Policy

Gradual normalization preserves relatively low yen funding costs.

Monitor:

- Policy decisions
- Forward guidance
- Outlook reports
- Wage and services inflation
- Financial-stability concerns

### Japanese Capital Flows

Pensions, insurers, banks, and households seek higher overseas returns.

Monitor:

- Portfolio-flow data
- Overseas security purchases
- Pension allocation
- Repatriation trends

### Global Risk Conditions

Severe stress can unwind carry trades and strengthen the yen rapidly.

Monitor:

- VIX
- Credit spreads
- Funding markets
- Global recession risk

## Core Assumptions

### A1 — Federal Reserve remains relatively restrictive

Wide rate differentials continue supporting dollar assets.

### A2 — Bank of Japan tightens gradually

Yen funding costs remain low relative to foreign yields.

### A3 — Carry trades remain profitable

Yield advantage exceeds volatility and funding costs.

### A4 — Japan avoids sustained intervention

Verbal intervention does not become repeated market action.

### A5 — Global markets remain broadly stable

No systemic shock forces widespread deleveraging.

### A6 — Japanese capital continues flowing overseas

Domestic investors retain incentives to own foreign assets.

## Thesis Strengtheners

- Delayed or reduced Fed easing
- Sticky U.S. inflation
- Gradual BOJ normalization
- Low market volatility
- Wider yield spreads
- Rising Japanese overseas investment

## Thesis Weakeners

- Aggressive Fed easing
- Faster BOJ tightening
- Persistent wage-driven Japanese inflation
- Carry-trade deleveraging
- Sustained currency intervention
- Capital repatriation
- Global financial stress

## Invalidation Conditions

Reassess if several occur together:

- Large Fed easing cycle
- Rapid BOJ tightening
- Meaningfully positive Japanese real rates
- Sustained carry unwind
- Structural reversal of Japanese capital outflows
- Long-duration foreign capital rotation into Japanese assets

## Important Constraint

Never conclude the thesis strengthened merely because USD/JPY increased.

Price is an outcome. Evidence must come from the causal drivers.
